UAE Embassy Attestation - Process Explained

About UAE Embassy Attestation

UAE Embassy Attestation is an essential process for verifying the authenticity of documents, ensuring they can be legally used in UAE. This process is required for various purposes, such as:

  • Obtaining employment visas
  • Pursuing higher education
  • Securing family visas
  • Setting up businesses
  • Conducting commercial transactions

The attestation process guarantees that documents issued in an individual‚Äôs home country are recognized by UAEi authorities. Here are the types of documents that require attestation:

  • Personal Documents:
    • Birth Certificates: For children's passports and school admissions
    • Marriage Certificates: For family visa applications
    • Divorce Certificates: For legal proof in remarriage or court proceedings
    • Death Certificates: For inheritance and visa closures
    • Police Clearance Certificates (PCC): For work visa applications and clean criminal records
    • Experience Certificates: For professional employment validation
  • Educational Documents: Degree certificates, diploma certificates, school leaving certificates for employment, and academic admissions.
  • Commercial Documents: Power of attorney, business registration papers, export invoices, trade licenses, no objection certificates (NOC) for job transfers, sponsorship modifications, or legal permissions.
