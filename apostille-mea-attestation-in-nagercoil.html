📄 Certificate Apostille – Process Explained

  • Identify the type of document—educational, personal, or commercial. It must be original and issued by an authorized institution.
  • Initial verification may be required, such as notarization or authentication by HRD, Home Department, or Chamber of Commerce.
  • Submit the verified document to the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) for the official apostille sticker or stamp.
  • Supporting documents like a passport copy may be needed. Ensure the certificate is unlaminated and intact.
  • The process typically takes a few working days and validates the document internationally for legal, academic, or professional use.
