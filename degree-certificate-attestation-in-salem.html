Degree Certificate Attestation - Process Explained

Degree certificate attestation is the process of verifying the authenticity of an educational degree for use abroad. It typically involves verification by the issuing educational institution, authentication by local authorities like the State HRD or Home Department, and final legalization by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) and the destination country’s embassy or consulate. This process is essential for employment, education, or immigration.

Types of Attestation for Degree Certificate

Notary Attestation: Verifies the authenticity of documents, signatures, and identities with a notary's seal, preventing fraud and ensuring legal recognition. This is the first step for embassy attestation.

HRD Attestation: Verified by the State HRD department to ensure authenticity for international studies or work.

SDM Attestation: Validates documents as an alternative to the State Home Department for international use.

MEA Attestation: The Ministry of External Affairs verifies documents for global purposes like employment, education, and migration.

Home Mantralaya Attestation: Verified by the State Home Department to ensure the authenticity of personal documents.

COC Attestation: Provided by the Chamber of Commerce to validate commercial documents, ensuring credibility and authenticity.
