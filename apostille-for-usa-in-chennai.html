What is an Apostille ? How to Get Apostille ?

An Apostille or an Authentication certifies the authenticity of the signature, seal and position of the official who has executed, issued or certified a copy of a public document. An Apostille or an Authentication enables a public document issued in one country to be recognized as valid in another country. While they accomplish the same objective, there are differences between Apostilles and Authentications.
The apostille process in India is typically used for personal documents such as birth certificates, marriage certificates, death certificates, affidavits, and educational documents like degrees, diplomas, and school certificates. The apostille verifies the authenticity of the document and ensures its acceptance in member countries without the need for further attestation or legalization.To obtain an apostille for an Indian-issued document, you would need to follow these general steps:
Notarization: The document may need to be notarized by a notary public or an authorized officer to verify its authenticity.
State Authentication: / SDM Authentication.After notarization, the document may need to be authenticated by the respective State Home Department or General Administrative Department. in some cases SDM -Sub Divisional Magistrate Attestation can be required.This step is done to verify the signature of the notary public.
Ministry of External Affairs (MEA): Once the document is authenticated by the State authority or SDM it needs to be submitted to the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) for apostille. The MEA will verify the authenticity of the document and issue the apostille.
