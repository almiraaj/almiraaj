About Birth Certificate Attestation

Birth Certificate Attestation is a crucial legal process that verifies the authenticity of a birth certificate for international use. It is often required by foreign governments and institutions for purposes such as higher education, employment, immigration, dependent visas, and legal documentation.

The attestation procedure typically involves validation by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) or Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA), followed by authentication from the respective embassy or consulate of the destination country. This ensures the document is genuine, legally recognized, and acceptable abroad.

Whether you're relocating for work, pursuing studies overseas, or applying for a family visa, Birth Certificate Attestation adds credibility and legal weight to your documentation, making it compliant with international standards.

For countries that are part of the Hague Convention, the process of document legalization is simplified through an Apostille. The Birth Certificate Apostille is a streamlined method of authentication accepted by over 120 countries, including the USA, UK, Germany, France, and Australia
