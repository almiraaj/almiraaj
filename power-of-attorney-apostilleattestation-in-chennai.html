What Is Power of Attorney Apostille?

Issuing a Power of Attorney allows someone to legally act on your behalf. It's commonly used for real estate, banking, or legal purposes. If you need your POA recognized in another country, apostille or embassy attestation is essential.

If your destination country is not part of the Hague Apostille Convention, additional embassy attestation is required.

Steps for Apostille & Attestation

  • Notary Public Attestation: Initial legal verification.
  • Home Department: Required for non-educational documents.
  • SDM Attestation: Sub Divisional Magistrate verification.
  • COC Attestation: For commercial POA involving business/legal documents.
  • MEA Apostille: Issued by the Ministry of External Affairs for Hague countries.
  • Embassy Attestation: Required for UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, and other non-Hague nations.
