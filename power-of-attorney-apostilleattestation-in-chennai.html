A Power of Attorney (POA) is a vital legal document that authorizes someone to act on your behalf for personal, property, or commercial matters. For NRIs and businesses, POA attestation is essential to ensure documents are legally recognized abroad. Without proper attestation, POAs may not be accepted by foreign authorities, banks, or courts. Common types of POA include:
  • General Power of Attorney – broad authority
  • Special Power of Attorney – limited to specific tasks
  • Durable Power of Attorney – valid even if incapacitated
  • Non‑Durable Power of Attorney – temporary authority
  • Medical Power of Attorney – healthcare decisions

The process involves:
  1. ✅ Notary Attestation – initial verification
  2. ✅ State Home Department authentication
  3. ✅ MEA Apostille/Attestation – Ministry of External Affairs
  4. ✅ Embassy Attestation – legalization by the destination country’s embassy
