Chinese Visa Process - Explained

Discover the breathtaking beauty and cultural richness of China, where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern marvels. Explore China with the Perfect Visa for Your Journey

  • Tourist Visa (L Visa): This visa is for those visiting China for tourism and sightseeing. It is generally valid for 30-60 days per entry and may allow single, double, or multiple entries. You might need to show proof of a return ticket and hotel reservation.

  • Business Visa (M Visa): For commercial and trade activities. Applicants often need an invitation letter from a Chinese business partner. The validity can range from 30 days to 1 year with single, double, or multiple entries, depending on the invitation.

  • Student Visa (X Visa): This visa is for students who have been admitted to a Chinese educational institution. There are two types: X1 for long-term studies (more than 180 days) and X2 for short-term studies (less than 180 days). You'll need a visa application form (JW201 or JW202) and an admission letter from the school.

  • Work Visa (Z Visa): Issued for those who have been offered employment in China. Applicants need a work permit or employment license from Chinese authorities. The Z visa is usually valid for 30 days after entering China, during which time you must apply for a residence permit to stay longer.

  • Family Reunion Visa (Q Visa): This visa is for family members of Chinese citizens or foreigners with permanent residence in China. Q1 is for long-term stays (more than 180 days) and Q2 is for short-term visits (up to 180 days). You will need an invitation letter from a Chinese citizen or a foreigner with a Chinese permanent residence permit, along with proof of the relationship.

Required Documents for L Tourist Visa for China (Indian National)

Document Description
Passport Must have at least 6 months of remaining validity and at least 2 blank visa pages.
Visa Application Form Complete the China Visa Application Form accurately and legibly.
Recent Photo Refer to the photo requirements for a recent passport-size color photo with a white background.
Round-trip Tickets and Hotel Bookings Proof of round-trip flight tickets and hotel reservations and day to itinerary
Invitation Letter An invitation letter from the inviter with details about your visit.
Bank Statement A recent bank statement showing your financial stability.
Employment Details If employed, a letter from your employer stating your leave of absence, purpose, and duration of the trip.
Travel Insurance Proof of travel insurance covering your stay in China.

Required Documents for Business Visa (M Visa) for China

Document Description
Passport Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months from the date of arrival in China and have at least one blank visa page.
Visa Application Form Complete the China Visa Application Form accurately and legibly.
Recent Photo A recent passport-size color photo with a white background. Refer to the photo requirements for more details.
Invitation Letter An invitation letter from a Chinese business or trade partner, or an invitation letter or confirmation letter of invitation issued by duly authorized Chinese institutes or relevant units.
Proof of Legal Stay or Residence If you are applying from a third country, provide proof of legal stay or residence.
Covering Letter A covering letter mentioning details of your visit, such as the purpose, duration, and places to be visited.
Previous Chinese Visas If applicable, provide copies of previous Chinese visas and the bio-page of your current and previous passports.
