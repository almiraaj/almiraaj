MEA Attestation Procedures in India

MEA Attestation refers to the legal authentication of documents by the Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India. It’s a crucial step when documents are to be used abroad for personal, academic, or commercial purposes.

The process ensures that the issued certificate or document is verified for its legitimacy and can be recognized internationally. MEA attestation is mandatory after State-level authentication (like HRD, Home Department, or Chamber of Commerce) for:

  • 🎓 Educational Documents: Degree, diploma, mark sheets
  • 👪 Personal Documents: Birth, marriage, divorce certificates
  • 🏢 Commercial Documents: Power of Attorney, bills, MOA, company certificates

The attestation process includes:

  1. ✅ Regional verification (HRD/Home/Chamber)
  2. ✅ MEA Attestation using adhesive stamp and seal
  3. ✅ Embassy Attestation (optional, if required)
