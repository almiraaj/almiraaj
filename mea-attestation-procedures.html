What is MEA Attestation?

MEA Attestation is the official verification of Indian documents by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA). It confirms the authenticity of educational, personal, and commercial certificates before they are legalized by foreign embassies or consulates. This step is mandatory for documents intended for use in non-Hague Convention countries.

Why is MEA Attestation Required?

MEA attestation ensures your documents are legally recognized abroad for purposes such as employment, higher education, migration, family visas, business setup, and legal procedures. Without MEA attestation, foreign authorities may reject your certificates, causing delays in your plans.

Step-by-Step MEA Attestation Process

The MEA attestation procedure generally includes:

  • Notary Attestation: Initial verification of the document
  • State Authentication: HRD, Home Department, or Chamber of Commerce depending on document type
  • MEA Attestation: Verification by the Ministry of External Affairs
  • Embassy Attestation: Legalization by the destination country‚Äôs Embassy/Consulate
  • MOFA Attestation: Final verification in the foreign country (if required)
