MEA Attestation Procedures in India

MEA Attestation refers to the legal authentication of documents by the Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India. It’s a crucial step when documents are to be used abroad for personal, academic, or commercial purposes.

MEA attestation ensures the document’s legitimacy and global recognition. It must follow State-level authentication (HRD, Home Department, or Chamber of Commerce), and is required for:

  • 🎓 Educational Certificates: Degrees, diplomas, mark sheets
  • 👪 Personal Documents: Birth, marriage, divorce certificates
  • 🏢 Commercial Documents: Power of Attorney, bills, company papers

The attestation process includes:

  1. ✅ Regional verification (HRD, Home Department, or Chamber of Commerce)
  2. ✅ MEA Attestation using adhesive stamp and official seal
  3. ✅ Embassy Attestation (if the destination country requires it)

📘 Frequently Asked Questions

What is MEA Attestation?

It is the official process of document authentication by the Ministry of External Affairs, validating prior verification for use in foreign countries.

Is it required for all international documents?

Yes—any Indian document used overseas for official, educational or legal purposes must undergo MEA attestation or apostille.
