Understanding unmarried certificate Apostille:

A single status certificate, also known as a certificate of no impediment or certificate of bachelorhood, is a legal document that confirms an individual's marital status as "single" or "unmarried." It serves as proof that the person mentioned in the certificate is not currently married and is free to enter into a legal marriage or civil partnership.The importance of a single status certificate can vary depending on the country and its specific legal requirements for marriage. Here are some common reasons why it is considered important:Marriage Abroad: If you plan to get married in a foreign country, the local authorities may require a single status certificate to ensure that you are not already married in your home country.Marriage License Application: In some jurisdictions, obtaining a marriage license requires proof of your single status. This certificate can be used as supporting documentation during the application process.Immigration Purposes: When someone from one country wishes to marry a citizen of another country, a single status certificate may be required as part of the immigration process to demonstrate eligibility for marriage.Spousal Visa Application: If you are applying for a spousal visa to join your partner in their country, authorities may ask for a single status certificate to ensure there are no legal barriers to your marriage.Avoiding Bigamy: The certificate helps prevent issues related to bigamy, which is the act of marrying someone while already legally married to another person. It provides assurance that the intending couple is free to marry each other.It's essential to check the specific requirements of the country or jurisdiction where you plan to marry, as the name, format, and process for obtaining a single status certificate may vary. Typically, the certificate can be obtained from the civil registration office, embassy, or consulate of your home country. It is advisable to apply for the certificate well in advance of your planned wedding date, as processing times can vary.

How to Get an Unmarried Certificate?

To get an Unmarried Certificate online in Tamil Nadu, you can follow these steps: Visit the TNeGA e-Sevai portal: Go to the Tamil Nadu e-Governance Agency (TNeGA) e-Sevai portal at https://www.tnesevai.tn.gov.in/.
2 . Register on the portal: If you haven't registered on the portal before, click on the "New User? Register Here" link and create an account. Provide the required details and complete the registration process.
Log in to the portal: Once registered, log in to the e-Sevai portal using your credentials. Select the Unmarried Certificate service: After logging in, select the "Unmarried Certificate" service from the list of available services. Fill in the application form: Fill in the required details in the online application form for the Unmarried Certificate. Provide accurate information and ensure that all mandatory fields are filled. Upload supporting documents: Upload the necessary supporting documents as specified in the application form. This may include your identification proof, address proof, and any other documents required. Pay the fee: Pay the prescribed fee for processing the Unmarried Certificate application. The fee amount will be mentioned on the portal. Submit the application: After completing the form and paying the fee, submit the application online. Track the application: You can track the status of your application on the e-Sevai portal using the application ID or registration number provided during the application process. Collect the certificate: Once your application is processed and approved, you will receive a notification. You can then download and print the Unmarried Certificate from the e-Sevai portal. It's important to note that the online process may require you to visit the concerned office in person for document verification or any additional steps. It's recommended to check the specific guidelines and instructions provided on the e-Sevai portal or contact the TNeGA helpline for any further assistance or clarification.

How to Get an Unmarried Affidavit ?

To get a Unmarried / Single Status Certificate in Tamil Nadu, you can follow these steps: Obtain an affidavit: Visit a notary or a lawyer to get an affidavit stating your single status. The affidavit should mention that you are unmarried and have never been married before. Get the affidavit notarized: Once you have the affidavit, get it notarized by a notary public. This step ensures the authenticity of the document. Submit the affidavit: Take the notarized affidavit to the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) office or the District Magistrate (DM) office in your area. Submit the affidavit along with the required documents. Provide supporting documents: Along with the affidavit, you may need to provide supporting documents such as proof of identity (passport, Aadhaar card, etc.), proof of address, and any other documents specified by the authorities. Pay the fee: Pay the required fee for processing the Single Status Certificate application. The fee amount may vary, so it's best to check with the SDM or DM office for the exact fee. Verification and processing: The authorities will verify the documents and process your application. This may take some time, so it's advisable to inquire about the estimated processing time. Collect the certificate: Once your application is processed and approved, you can collect the Single Status Certificate from the SDM or DM office. Make sure to carry the necessary identification documents for verification.
