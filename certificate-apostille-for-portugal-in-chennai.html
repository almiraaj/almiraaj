About Apostille Certificate for Portugal

An Apostille or an Authentication certifies the authenticity of the signature, seal and position of the official who has executed, issued or certified a copy of a public document. An Apostille or an Authentication enables a public document issued in one country to be recognized as valid in another country. While they accomplish the same objective, there are differences between Apostilles and Authentications.An Apostille is used as proof that your documents can be accepted as authentic by Portuguese law authorities; an apostille stamp provides evidence that their validity has been assessed accurately by an authoritative body within that nation.

These additional authentication requirements may include:Portugese Translation: If your documents are in a language other than English or Portugese, you will likely need to provide a certified translation of the documents into either English or Portugese. The translation should be done by a professional translator who is recognized by the embassy.Educational Documents: For educational certificates and academic qualifications, you may need to provide the original degree certificates, mark sheets, and transcripts. The embassy may also require verification from the educational institution that issued the documents.Commercial Documents: For commercial documents such as business contracts, invoices, or certificates of incorporation, you may need to provide notarized copies of these documents, along with any supporting documentation required by the embassy.Personal Documents: For personal documents such as marriage certificates, birth certificates, and affidavits, you will need to provide the original documents along with notarized copies.
