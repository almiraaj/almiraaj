About Single Status Certificate:

A Single Status Certificate, also known as a Certificate of No Impediment or Certificate of Non-Impediment, is an official document that confirms an individual's marital status as single and legally free to marry. It serves as proof that the person is not currently married or involved in any existing marriage or civil partnership.The certificate is typically issued by the government authorities in the country where the individual is a citizen or a legal resident. It may be required when getting married abroad or applying for a marriage visa or spousal visa in another country.
To obtain a Single Status Certificate, the individual usually needs to provide certain documents and information, such as passport, birth certificate, and proof of address. The process and requirements for obtaining the certificate may vary depending on the country or jurisdiction issuing it.Once issued, the Single Status Certificate is typically valid for a specific period, and it may need to be apostilled or legalized for use in a foreign country, especially if the country where the marriage will take place is a signatory to the Hague Apostille Convention.
The Single Status Certificate plays a crucial role in international marriages, ensuring that the intended spouses meet the legal requirements to marry each other and preventing any legal complications arising from existing marriages in other countries.

Some other names for Single Status Certificate

A Single Status Certificate, which confirms an individual's marital status as single and eligible for marriage, goes by various names in different places around the world. Here are some alternate names for a Single Status Certificate in various regions: Certificate of No Impediment (CNI): This name is commonly used in the United Kingdom and some other Commonwealth countries. Certificate of No Record of Marriage (CENOMAR): This term is used in the Philippines. Certificate of No Marriage Record (CNMR): Similar to CENOMAR, this name is used in some countries like the Philippines. Single Status Affidavit: This term is frequently used in the United States and some other countries. Certificate of Non-Impediment: This name is used in some European countries and is often abbreviated as CNI. Unmarried Certificate: In some places, a simple term like "unmarried certificate" may be used to indicate the same document. Certificate of Freedom to Marry: This name is used in some countries to denote the document that confirms an individual's eligibility for marriage. Singlehood Certificate: In some regions, this name is used to refer to the document that attests to an individual's single status. It's important to note that the specific name and format of the document may vary from country to country, and the requirements for obtaining it may also differ. Some countries may require additional documentation or procedures to issue the certificate. If you need a Single Status Certificate for a specific purpose, it is essential to inquire with the relevant government authorities or the embassy/consulate of the country where you need to use the certificate to understand the specific requirements and process for obtaining it.
