Apostille in Unmarried certificate


A single status certificate, also known as a Certificate of No Impediment or Certificate of Non-Impediment, is an official document that confirms an individual's marital status as single and legally free to marry. It serves as proof that the person is not currently married or involved in any existing marriage or civil partnership.The certificate is typically issued by the government authorities in the country where the individual is a citizen or a legal resident. It may be required when getting married abroad or applying for a marriage visa or spousal visa in another country.
To obtain a single status certificate, the individual usually needs to provide certain documents and information, such as passport, birth certificate, and proof of address. The process and requirements for obtaining the certificate may vary depending on the country or jurisdiction issuing it.Once issued, the single status certificate is typically valid for a specific period, and it may need to be apostilled or legalized for use in a foreign country, especially if the country where the marriage will take place is a signatory to the Hague Apostille Convention.
The single status certificate plays a crucial role in international marriages, ensuring that the intended spouses meet the legal requirements to marry each other and preventing any legal complications arising from existing marriages in other countries.

Importance of Apostille in Single Status Certificate:

A Single Status Certificate, also sometimes known as a singleness certificate, is simply put, a certificate proving one’s bachelorhood. Or in other words, a legal document that specifies one’s marital status as per the judiciary laws of the country. Unlike an official document, it is a self sworn statement stating that the affiant is single, or has no dependents (in case of a divorce) from any previous marriages. This provides the affiant with the license to tie the knot in any foreign country.Be it India, China, US, UK or any other country, transnational marriages are pretty common these days. India is the largest source country for international migrants globally and with a well over 15 million Indians settled abroad, it is quite inevitable that a large number of them end up marrying from other countries. However, the path to doing this isn’t a cakewalk and requires something called a Single Status Certificate.
