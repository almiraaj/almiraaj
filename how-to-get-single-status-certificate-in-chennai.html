what is single status certificate?

A single status certificate, also known as a certificate of no impediment or certificate of no record, is a document issued by the government proving that an individual is currently not married. People usually need this certificate to get married abroad or for other legal matters where proof of marital status is required.

what are the other names for single status certificate?

It's known by several names, including:Certificate of No Impediment,Certificate of Freedom to Marry,Affidavit of Single Status,Certificate of No Record,Certificate of No Marriage. Certificate of No Record of Marriage (CENOMAR)Certificate of No Marriage Record (CNMR) Similar to CENOMAR, this name is used in some countries like the Philippines. also known by Single Status Affidavit ,Certificate of Non-Impediment,Unmarried Certificate.

what is the importance of getting apostille in a single status certificate?

As the apostille is a form of authentication issued to documents for use in countries that participate in the Hague Convention of 1961. When you get your single status certificate apostilled, it means that the document has been certified for international use. This can be especially important if you're planning to get married abroad.

how to get an unmarried certificate in india ?

To get an unmarried certificate (also known as a single status certificate) in India, you can follow these steps:Apply for unmarried certificate.Have your single status certificate attested by either the State Home Department or the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) .Apostille by MEA: Submit the attested affidavit to the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) for apostille, which certifies it for international use.Embassy Attestation (if required): If the country you're planning to go to isn't part of the Hague Convention, you may need additional attestation from the respective embassy.Make sure to carry necessary documents like your address proof, birth proof, and parental confirmation.
