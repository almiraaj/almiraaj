What is Death Certificate Apostille?

An apostille verifies the authenticity of a public document like a death certificate for use in countries that are members of the Hague Convention. It eliminates the need for further legalization by embassies.

Why is it Required?

  • Claiming inheritance or property overseas
  • Settling pension, life insurance, or financial dues
  • Closing international bank accounts
  • Filing legal documents in foreign jurisdictions

Process (Hague Countries)

  1. Notary Attestation
  2. State Home Department or SDM Authentication
  3. MEA Apostille from the Ministry of External Affairs

For Non-Hague Countries (Embassy Attestation)

  1. Notary Attestation
  2. State Home Department Attestation
  3. MEA Attestation
  4. Embassy/Consulate Attestation

Validity & Duration

Validity: Generally permanent unless country-specific timelines apply.
Processing Time: 3–7 business days depending on the state of issuance and document status.

Required Documents

  • Original Death Certificate
  • Passport copy of next of kin
  • Authorization letter if submitted via agent
