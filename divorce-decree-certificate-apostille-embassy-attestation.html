Understanding Divorce Documents

Divorce Decree: A court-issued document detailing the terms of divorce—custody, alimony, property division.
Divorce Certificate: A government-issued proof that the marriage has legally ended, used for administrative purposes.

What Is Apostille & Attestation?

  • Apostille: International legalization under the Hague Convention for countries that recognize it.
  • Attestation: Embassy or consulate verification for non-Hague countries.
  • Purpose: Required for immigration, remarriage abroad, or legal proceedings overseas.
  • Process: Includes notarization, state authentication, and final legalization.
  • Duration: Typically 5–15 working days depending on destination country.
  • Importance: Without proper legalization, your documents may be rejected internationally.
