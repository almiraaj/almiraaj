Introduction

While death marks the end of a life, it often begins a journey of formalities for those left behind.

For families with international ties—especially NRIs—death certificate attestation ensures recognition abroad for claims, assets, and obligations.

What Is Death Certificate Attestation?

It is the official validation of a government-issued death certificate by MEA and foreign embassies, confirming its authenticity for international use.

Why Is It Required?

  • Claim insurance or pension abroad
  • Transfer international assets
  • Settle inheritance matters
  • Close foreign accounts
  • Repatriate remains
  • Apply for family visas

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Notarization
  2. State or SDM Authentication
  3. MEA Validation
  4. Embassy Attestation

Embassy Attestation Countries

Includes UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman, Bahrain, Egypt, Nigeria, Vietnam, China, and more.

Apostille Countries

Includes USA, Canada, Germany, France, Japan, South Korea, Australia, and other Hague Convention members.
