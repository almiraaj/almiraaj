While death marks the end of a life, it often begins a journey of formalities for those left behind.

For families with international ties—especially Non-Resident Indians (NRIs)—death certificate attestation is a vital legal process. It ensures that the certificate issued in India is recognized abroad for settling claims, transferring assets, and fulfilling final obligations.

What Is Death Certificate Attestation?

It is the official validation of a government-issued death certificate by authorized bodies, including the Ministry of External Affairs and the destination country’s embassy. This process confirms the authenticity of the certificate for use in foreign jurisdictions.

Why Is It Required?

  • Claim life insurance or pension benefits abroad
  • Transfer international assets or property
  • Settle inheritance or succession matters
  • Close foreign bank accounts and investments
  • Repatriate remains or conduct last rites
  • Apply for family visas or dependent benefits

Step-by-Step Attestation Process

  1. Notarization – Initial verification by a licensed notary
  2. State Home Department Authentication – Endorsement by the issuing state
  3. Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) – Central government validation
  4. Embassy Attestation – Final approval by the destination country’s embassy
