Hague Convention Apostille Countries List

The Hague Convention of 1961 simplifies the legalization of documents for international use. Countries that are part of this convention accept apostilled documents without further embassy attestation.

For the official list of member countries, visit the Hague Convention Members Page .

Frequently Asked Questions on Apostille for Indians

Indians usually need apostille for birth certificates, marriage certificates, education certificates (degree, diploma, transcripts), and commercial documents when applying for jobs, higher studies, or business abroad.

Popular Hague Convention countries for Indians include USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, France, Italy, Netherlands, Spain, and Singapore.
