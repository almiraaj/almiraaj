About Apostille For Greece

Apostille is a standardized legalization process under the Hague Convention of 1961. For Greece, Apostille ensures that Indian‑issued certificates are recognized without further embassy attestation.

Whether it’s educational certificates, unmarried certificates, or police clearance certificates (PCC), Apostille is mandatory for visa, employment, higher studies, marriage, and immigration purposes in Greece.

Why is Apostille Required For Greece?

Apostille validates your Indian documents for direct acceptance in Greece. It is essential for:

  • 🎓 Admission to Greece universities
  • 💼 Employment opportunities in Greece
  • 💍 Marriage registration abroad
  • 🏠 Immigration and residency processes
  • ✈️ Visa applications for travel, work, or study

Step-by-Step Apostille Process

The Apostille process For Greece typically involves:

  • Notary Attestation: Initial verification of the document
  • State Home Department / HRD: Authentication at the state level
  • MEA Apostille: Ministry of External Affairs provides the Apostille sticker/seal

Once Apostilled, your document is directly valid in Greece without the need for further embassy attestation.

Documents Commonly Apostilled For Greece
