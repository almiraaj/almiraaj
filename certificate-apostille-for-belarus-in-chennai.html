What is Certificate Apostille?

Certificate Apostille is the process of verifying and authenticating documents such as personal, educational, and commercial certificates so they are legally recognized in Belarus and other Hague Convention countries. This ensures your papers are valid for study, work, marriage, or business abroad.

Why is Apostille Important?

Apostille ensures your certificates are accepted by foreign governments, universities, and employers. It is crucial for higher education admissions, overseas employment, marriage registration, and smooth international transactions.

Apostille vs Attestation

Apostille is used for Hague Convention countries like Belarus and involves a single government stamp. Attestation is a multi-step process required for non-Hague countries involving state, MEA, and embassy verification.

  • Apostille : For Hague Convention countries (e.g., Belarus, France, Germany)
  • Attestation : For non-Hague countries (e.g., UAE, Saudi Arabia)

Step-by-Step Process of Apostille

Apostille of certificates involves multiple verification stages to ensure authenticity and global acceptance. The process typically includes:

  1. State Verification: Initial verification of personal, educational, or commercial documents.
  2. MEA (Ministry of External Affairs): Central government authentication.
  3. Apostille Stamp: Final legalization for Belarus and other Hague Convention countries.
