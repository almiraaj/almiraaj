What Is a Legal Heir Certificate?

A Legal Heir Certificate is a government-issued document that identifies the rightful successors of a deceased person. It is essential for:

  • Property inheritance and ownership transfer
  • Bank account closure and fund claims
  • Insurance settlements and pension claims
  • OCI/NRI inheritance documentation

Why Apostille or Attestation Is Required

To use your Legal Heir Certificate abroad, it must be authenticated through MEA Apostille or Embassy Attestation. This ensures:

  • ✔️ Legal recognition in foreign countries
  • ✔️ Acceptance by embassies and consulates
  • ✔️ Validity for visa, property, and legal claims

Step-by-Step Attestation Process

  1. Obtain the Legal Heir Certificate from local authorities.
  2. Get it notarized and verified by the State Home Department or SDM.
  3. Submit for MEA Apostille or Embassy Attestation.

Who Can Apply?

  • ✔️ Spouse of the deceased
  • ✔️ Children (son/daughter)
  • ✔️ Parents or siblings (if no spouse or children)
  • ✔️ OCI/NRI family members with valid proof

Documents Required

  • ✔️ Original Legal Heir Certificate
  • ✔️ Passport copy of applicant
  • ✔️ Death certificate of the deceased
  • ✔️ Relationship proof (Aadhaar, birth certificate, etc.)
  • ✔️ Embassy-specific forms if applicable

Frequently Asked Questions

Is attestation mandatory for legal heir certificates?

Yes, if you plan to use the certificate abroad, attestation or apostille is required for legal validity.

How long does the attestation process take?

Typically 5–10 working days, depending on the country and document type.

Can NRIs apply for legal heir certificate attestation?

Absolutely. NRIs and OCI holders can apply through authorized agents like ALMIRAAJ International.

What if the deceased left no will?

The legal heir certificate serves as proof of succession in the absence of a will.
