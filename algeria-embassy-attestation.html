Algeria Embassy Attestation Services in India

Algeria is not a member of the Hague Apostille Convention, so it follows the attestation process, not apostille.That means if you're submitting documents for use in Algeria—whether for work, study, or legal purposes—they must go through a multi-step embassy attestation process.

Who Needs Algeria Embassy Attestation?

  • Professionals taking up jobs in Algeria
  • Students applying for educational institutions
  • Individuals entering into marriage registration or family visa processes
  • Entrepreneurs starting business ventures or setting up branches
  • Persons involved in legal, real estate, or immigration procedures

Documents We Attest

  • Educational: Degree, Diploma, SSC, HSC certificates
  • Personal: Birth, Marriage, Death Certificates, PCC
  • Commercial: Invoices, MOA, POA, Company Registration & Trade Licenses

Procedure for Algeria Attestation

  1. Notary Public Attestation
  2. Home Department or SDM Attestation
  3. MEA (Ministry of External Affairs) Attestation
  4. Algeria Embassy Attestation (final legalization)
