Power of Attorney Apostille & Attestation Services

A Power of Attorney (PoA) is a legally binding document that authorizes another person to act on your behalf in personal, financial, property, or legal matters. It's especially useful for overseas transactions, handling properties, or managing business when you're not physically present.

Types of Power of Attorney

  • General PoA: Grants broad powers for managing property, banking, or legal affairs.
  • Special or Limited PoA: Used for a specific task or transaction, such as property registration or legal signing.
  • Durable PoA: Remains valid even if the person issuing it becomes incapacitated.
  • Medical PoA: Appoints someone to make healthcare decisions on your behalf in medical scenarios.

Why Legalize a PoA?

For your Power of Attorney to be accepted internationally, it must undergo legalization through either apostille or embassy attestation, depending on the destination country‚Äôs legal system.
