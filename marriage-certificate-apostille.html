Marriage Certificate Apostille is a legal authentication process that validates your marriage certificate for use in countries that are part of the Hague Convention. It is issued by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) in India and is essential for international recognition of marital status, especially for spouse visa applications, family reunification, and legal rights abroad.

Before apostille, your marriage certificate may require marriage certificate attestation, which involves verification by local notary, state authorities, and the Ministry of External Affairs. This step ensures the document is legally recognized for international use, especially in non-apostille countries.
