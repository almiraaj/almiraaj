What is Affidavit Apostille & Attestation?

Affidavit Apostille and Attestation is the process of verifying the authenticity of sworn statements or declarations issued in India. These documents are often required for visa applications, property claims, inheritance, business agreements, and family-related legal matters abroad. Apostille is valid for Hague Convention countries, while attestation is required for non-Hague countries.

Why is Affidavit Attestation Important?

Attestation ensures your affidavit is legally recognized by foreign governments, embassies, and institutions. Without proper attestation, your affidavit may be rejected abroad, causing delays in immigration, property transfers, inheritance claims, or business transactions.

Apostille vs Attestation

Apostille is a simplified legalization process for Hague Convention countries, involving a single government stamp. Attestation is a multi-step procedure required for non-Hague countries, involving Notary, State Authentication, MEA verification, and Embassy legalization.

  • Apostille : For Hague Convention countries (e.g., France, Germany, Italy)
  • Attestation : For non-Hague countries (e.g., UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar)
